## 02 — Personalized Tutor & Study Companion

(2024)* `[gov/peer-reviewed — preprint + reputable coverage]`

## Cost-of-doing-nothing models (arithmetic shown — defensible, not invented)

> Every dollar figure below is a **model** built from two or more cited inputs. The figures are **illustrative at a stated baseline**; on a real slide, substitute the customer's actual volumes. Where a single published "$ per X" exists (e.g., teacher turnover, transfer credit loss), the model is unnecessary and the direct figure is used instead.

- **01 Concierge — avoidable contact-handling cost.** `120,000 routine contacts/yr × 60% deflectable × ~$16 avg. cost delta (phone/email $17–$25/$8–$15 → self-service $1–$4) ≈ $1.15M/yr.` Inputs: cost-per-contact benchmarks (InvGate/industry, 2024–25, `[sector-press/estimate]`) × a stated institutional contact volume. Conservative because it counts only clearly-routine contacts.
- **02 Tutor — human-tutoring substitution cost.** `1,000 students × $1,200–$2,500/student/yr (high-impact tutoring) = $1.2M–$2.5M+/yr.` Inputs: high-impact-tutoring unit cost (EdResearch for Action / Nickow et al., 2024, `[gov/peer-reviewed + foundation/research]`) × served population. Frames AI tutor as the scalable alternative to either that spend or no coverage.
- **04 Assessment — recovered grading labor.** `9.9 grading hrs/wk × 36 wks = ~356 hrs/yr/instructor; × 30% reduction × ~$40/hr loaded = ~$4,270/instructor/yr.` Inputs: grading-hours survey (Learnosity 2025, `[sector-press]`) × conservative reduction × loaded instructional rate. Scale across teaching faculty.
- **05 Student Success — forgone tuition from non-return.** `2,500 first-years × 22.4% non-return = 560 lost/yr; × ~$10,000 net tuition+fees/student-yr = ~$5.6M/yr` (lower-bound per-student revenue loss; 4-yr higher). ## Labeling discipline (read before building any slide)

1. **Lead with the strongest class.** Headline each slide with a `[gov/peer-reviewed]` or `[foundation/research]` figure (GAO, NCES/IPEDS, NSC, RAND, CCRC, EDUCAUSE, Tyton, Learning Policy Institute, peer-reviewed RCTs). Use vendor and industry-estimate figures only as supporting color.
2. **Flag vendor figures on-slide.** Any `[vendor]` stat (including AWS customer stories and tool-maker efficiency claims) must be visibly attributed to the named institution/vendor on the slide itself — never presented as an independent benchmark.
3. **Flag industry estimates and dated figures.** Cost-per-ticket/contact ranges, the "40% of teaching time on grading" figure, the GAO-17 credit-loss numbers, and NACADA caseloads are either industry estimates or pre-2024; label them as such.
4. **Outcomes are modeled, not promised.** Present ROI as "documented at [institution]" or "modeled for a reference institution at your baseline," not as a guarantee.
5. **Prefer the primary URL.** Where a secondary source (press) summarizes a primary study (GAO/RAND/NSC/peer-review), cite both and link the primary.
